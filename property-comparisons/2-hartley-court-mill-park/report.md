## Quick Verdict

2 Hartley is the best functional match among the new Mill Park candidates. It gives four bedrooms, two bathrooms, two living zones, an upstairs retreat, a proper family/meals zone, a double garage and a covered alfresco. Photos show a cleaner, more modernised interior than Archer/Streeton/Berala.

The tradeoff is auction price risk and outdoor maintenance. If bidding stays below the high $800s, it could be a serious contender. If competition pushes it into the low $900s, it starts to stress your comfort band and should be compared hard against every stronger-known benchmark.

**AI fit score:** 82 / 100  
**Price-risk score:** 5 / 10  
**Fair value estimate:** $840k-$910k  
**Likely transaction range:** $860k-$950k  
**Practical read:** Inspect seriously, but keep a hard ceiling.

## Photo Read

The photos show a clean, modernised house rather than a major project. Kitchen and bathrooms look more current than the older timber-kitchen stock. The main living/meals/kitchen zone opens directly to the alfresco, which matches your preference for easy daily use. Bedrooms look brighter and more generous than several other Mill Park options.

The caution is that the lawn and garden still need work. This is not the compact low-maintenance Buick/Northbridge style.

## Price Lens

Auction dynamics are the main risk. For Mill Park, the recent 5 Berala sale at $820k is a useful older-house comp, but Hartley appears more spacious and modernised, so it can justify a premium. The question is how much premium is still rational.
