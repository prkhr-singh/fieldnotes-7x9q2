## Quick Verdict

This is a polished, low-maintenance Mill Park house with a stronger finish level than many older 3-bed options. The renovated kitchen, clean bathrooms, double garage, compact 355m2 block and single-level layout are all practical positives.

The two issues are decisive: there is no dedicated study, and the post-auction asking level now looks high for a 3-bedroom home without a fourth bedroom/retreat. It is more refined than 77 Prince Of Wales, but 77 has the better WFH layout and a lower price expectation. Woodhill is worth watching only if the price softens materially.

## Photo And Floorplan Read

Photos read as clean and sale-ready: renovated kitchen, hybrid flooring, plantation shutters, downlights, neutral bathrooms, roller shutters and a tidy facade. The home feels more turnkey than the older brick value stock. The double garage with internal access is a major functional plus over carport-heavy homes.

The floorplan is efficient but not expansive. It is essentially a comfortable 3-bed single-level home with one main living/dining/kitchen zone. There is no second living area, study nook, retreat or spare flex room. For a homebody/WFH household, that means the layout depends heavily on whether one of the secondary bedrooms can be permanently sacrificed as an office.

## Price Lens

At the original $700k-$770k auction guide, this would have been a serious value candidate. At the later $765k-$835k mirror guide, it became a reasonable but not obvious target. At an observed $849,500 asking price, the value case weakens.

The closest calibration points:

- 5 Berala Place sold for $820k with 4 bedrooms plus study on 575m2. Woodhill is more polished and lower maintenance, but less flexible.
- 77 Prince Of Wales is now around $760k-$800k / $810k asking evidence and has a real office and WIP, though it is older and carport-heavy.
- 1 Buick Crescent remains the best lifestyle benchmark because it has the standout retreat/study and covered alfresco.

Do not pay a premium price for a 3-bed home that still needs a bedroom to become the office.

## Risks And Unknowns

- Current asking-price position needs agent confirmation after the failed/paused auction campaign.
- No dedicated study or retreat.
- Only one living zone; no redundancy if WFH, guests and future child needs overlap.
- Confirm land/title, any easements, and whether the compact side/rear yards feel private enough.
- Check renovation quality: kitchen joinery, bathroom waterproofing, flooring transitions, roller shutters and split-system age.
- Verify whether the apparent low-maintenance outdoor areas are covered/useful or just narrow paved side yards.

## Bottom Line

Watch, but do not chase. Woodhill is an easy-to-like, low-maintenance house, but at about $849k it is too expensive for a no-study 3-bed layout. It becomes interesting only if negotiation brings it back toward the low-$800s and inspection confirms the finish is genuinely strong.
