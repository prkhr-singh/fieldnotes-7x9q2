## First Impression

This property produced the strongest and most divided in-person reaction of the day. Both buyers liked it equally — the first property to achieve genuine mutual appeal. But the rational and emotional reads pulled in opposite directions.

**Emotional read:** Could become home. Strong character throughout. Thoughtful design — large elements placed very well. Self-sufficient with usable space across levels and outdoor. The kind of house where both people could see themselves living.

**Rational read:** Built 1983 (pre-1987 — asbestos risk window). On market since January (5+ months). Previously listed above $900k. Crowded street (many cars suggesting density or local activity). Area quality felt weaker than Highton. At ~$805k it is expensive relative to alternatives with fewer unknowns. Move-in readiness for 5 comfortable years is questionable.

The emotional reaction is real and meaningful. So is the rational concern. Both should be held.

**Living room:** Nice. Felt good even before applying extra mental credit. Strong character in the living zones.

**Upstairs space:** Confirmed multi-level layout — usable space upstairs, downstairs, and outside. The upstairs component adds flexibility for WFH, guests, or future use cases.

**Heating / cooling:** Split-system AC present. No ducted noted.

**Upgrades by current owners (3 years):**

- Watering systems installed
- Small shed constructed under outdoor area

**Construction era (confirmed):** Built 1983 — agent confirmed. This is pre-1987 and sits squarely in the asbestos risk window. Garage lining, soffits, eaves, bathroom backing tiles and sub-floor sheeting are all candidate materials. Independent B&P plus hygienist survey if ACM flagged.
