## Contract Review: Owner-Builder Red Flags

This is the gating issue. The contract contains a direct internal contradiction:

**The contradiction:**
- Vendor has marked "owner builder: not applicable"
- Vendor has marked "building permits in the preceding 7 years: not applicable"
- Yet a **separate owner-builder defect report exists** in the contract documents

If the vendor is not an owner-builder and no permits were pulled in the last 7 years, there should be no owner-builder defect report. Its presence means either:
1. Works were done on an owner-builder basis and the vendor disclosure is incomplete; or
2. Some works were completed without council permits, and the defect report was obtained as a substitute for formal sign-off.

Building permit numbers and certificates were noted as not available in the contract.

**Specific defects recorded:**
- Bathroom window glass — may not be safety-grade (AS 1288 compliance question)
- Kitchen range hood / ceiling ventilation — not ducted correctly to roof exterior (building code compliance issue)
- Structural/landing-related inaccessible areas — could not be fully assessed
- Zero access to roof void — no inspection possible of roof framing, insulation, or any services above ceiling level

**Implications:**
- Finance clause is strongly recommended — lender's valuer may flag the permit gap
- Independent B&P and structural integrity inspection is non-negotiable before exchange
- Timber pest inspection required (1965 timber-frame era)
- If specific defects are not remediated by vendor: obtain quotes and deduct from offer
- Some unpermitted work can be regularised retrospectively (council retrospective approval) — but this requires investigation and adds timeline risk
