## 4. Owner-Builder Works — The Core Risk

### 4.1 What happened

The Section 32 marks "Owner Builder: Not Applicable" and "Building Permits preceding 7 years: Not Applicable." However, the contract includes a 6-page **Owner Builder Inspection Report** (Sherlock Inspections / Sherspec Ballarat Pty Ltd, Rudolf Venema, IN-L 38425, report dated 5/05/2026). The owner-builders named in the report are **Hamish & Rebecca Rose**, the vendors themselves.

The report was prepared under section 137B(2)(a) of the Building Act 1993 "for use in assessing a proposal for warranty insurance."

### 4.2 Works covered by the owner-builder report

| Item | Works |
|---|---|
| a | Deck attached to rear of existing dwelling |

| e | Bathroom renovation (vanity, basin, shower, bath, splash tiles, fixtures, floor tiles) |
| f | En-suite renovation (vanity, tiled shower recess, toilet, splash tiles, fixtures, floor tiles) |
| g | Laundry renovation (cabinet, benchtop, splashback, fixtures, floor tiles) |
| h | Toilet renovation (pan, cistern, floor tiles) |

**Note:** Inspector confirmed — "The owner builder reported that no structural alterations were made to the dwelling."

### 4.3 Building permit documentation

| Document | Status |
|---|---|
| Building permit number | "Permit not available at the time of the inspection" |
| Certificate of Final Inspection | "Not available" |
| Drawings | Not available |
| Drawings stamped by building surveyor | -- |
| Compliance certificates (waterproofing etc.) | Not sighted |

**There are no building permits for any of these works.** The adequacy of the structural works (deck, office addition, shed roof) could not be verified by the inspector because drawings were not available.

### 4.4 The legal ambiguity

The vendor's conveyancer has marked "Owner Builder: Not Applicable" in the Section 32. The most likely interpretation is that the works were completed more than 6 years before listing (i.e., before approximately 2020), which would take them outside the s137B obligation window. However:
- The report was commissioned and signed on 5 May 2026 (11 days before inspection)
- No warranty insurance certificate has been included in the contract

**Open question for your conveyancer or Ricky:** When were the works completed? If within the preceding 6 years, the vendor must obtain warranty insurance under s137B before selling. If genuinely more than 6 years ago, "Not Applicable" is correct but the works still have no permit documentation.

## 10. B&P Scope — Mandatory Before Exchange

The Sherlock Inspections report is NOT a pre-purchase inspection. A full B&P (AS 4349.1) must be commissioned with the following explicit scope:

- Asbestos survey — 1965 brick veneer construction; eaves, internal wall linings, roof space, outbuildings, and any fibro/AC sheeting
- Timber pest inspection — termite risk in 1965 timber-frame structure
- Wet area waterproofing — behind all tiled wet areas (bathroom, en-suite, laundry); compliance certificates were not sighted; en-suite sealant mould is a warning sign
- Roof void inspection — owner-builder inspector waived this; B&P inspector should access with appropriate equipment
- Deck structural integrity — no permits, no engineering sign-off; verify footings and bearer adequacy
- Office addition structural integrity — no permits; concrete slab footing confirmed but adequacy unverified
- Shed roof — insufficient screws and incomplete gutters; assess for wind uplift risk
- Subfloor (where accessible) — moisture, timber condition, drainage
- All 12 disclosed defects — assess remediation requirements and costs
