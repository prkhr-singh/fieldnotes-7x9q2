# In-Person Assessment: 31 St Hellier Street, Heidelberg Heights

Note: AI transcription app recorded this auction as "31 Canterbury Street" — believed to be a transcription error. All details match this property (agent Paul, Banyule Council, 3-bed/2-bath/single-level, same auction date and guide).

## First Impression

Mixed. No major obvious flaws but no strong positive pull either. Felt "fine" in parts but not compelling.

## Room-by-Room Notes

**Master bedroom (3.1 × 3.5m = 10.9m²):** Felt small. The listed dimensions told the story — under 11m² is noticeably compact for a master. Only a built-in robe, not a walk-in. This contributed significantly to the sense that the house didn't fit their stage of life.

**Ensuite:** Specifically noted as "notably small." Renovated and clean (matching listing photos) but the compact dimensions were felt in person. No walk-in robe equivalent.

**Bedroom 2 / 3:** Not specifically noted. Bedroom 3 (7.5m² = 2.5 × 3.0m) is borderline usable as a second bedroom for an adult. Effectively turns the house into a 2-bedder for their use.

**Living room:** Felt small. Despite having a separate lounge (3.9 × 5.4m) and dining/living (3.2 × 4.1m), the single-level layout and overall floor plate (~105–120m²) created a cramped impression.

**Kitchen:** Not specifically noted negatively — the AI scored it 9/10 for kitchen storage and that is likely accurate. But kitchen quality alone doesn't rescue the overall layout.

**Laundry:** Not mentioned. Likely embedded in the bathroom/utility zone as typical for single-level 1970s layout.

**Rear area / deck:** The main positive. Open timber deck with firepit. But: personal lifestyle mismatch confirmed — "would never naturally sit in that kind of rear area." The outdoor area is attractive in listing photos but didn't create an emotional pull. Seasonal limitation (~180 days in Melbourne) and the fact that you could build a similar deck elsewhere for less both weakened the case.

**Outdoor seating (back):** "Distance from the house made it hard to judge how usable that sitting area would be." Detached/separate sitting area — not adjacent or covered.

## What AI Got Right

- No study (scored 5/10) — confirmed. There is nowhere to work at home without sacrificing a bedroom.
- Room spaciousness scored 6/10 — confirmed. Master especially. Single-level floor plate can't generate the floor area a two-storey can.
- Location scored high (9/10 for location fit, not in standard rubric) — Heidelberg Heights IS well-located for wife's anchors. That part is accurate.
- Low maintenance outdoor scored 7/10 — garden requires more care than Buick's alfresco. Confirmed by the firepit/deck setup.

## What AI Got Wrong / Missed

1. **"In-person feel of small" for the master bedroom:** 10.9m² looked acceptable on a floor plan; it felt noticeably small in person. Floor plans can't convey this well.
2. **Rear outdoor area lifestyle mismatch:** AI scored low_maintenance at 7/10 based on garden size and maintenance effort. It couldn't predict that the detached outdoor seating area would not match how they actually use outdoor space. The score missed the habit-fit dimension entirely.
3. **Single-storey floor plate impression:** AI scored individual rooms. In person, the cumulative effect of a ~110m² single-storey floor plate felt more cramped than the individual room scores suggested.

## Lifestyle Fit

- **WFH days:** Poor. No study. Would have to use Bedroom 3 (7.5m²) or dining table. Effectively loses a bedroom.
- **Morning routine:** Neutral. Single-level is convenient in some ways.
- **Weekend:** Rear deck attractive in theory but personal habit mismatch.
- **Hosting guests:** Limited by Bedroom 3 size.
- **Long-term (5–10 years):** Better suited for empty-nesters. The winner at auction appeared to be a late-life couple — 10-year horizon before reselling. For young families, it's too constrained.

## Competing Buyer Profile

Auction was competitive. Winner appeared to be a later-life couple (children moved out, empty-nester stage). For that profile, ~$1M makes sense. For buyers with growing family needs, the small rooms and no study make $800k the ceiling.

Crowd was active. Bidding opened at $860k (silence at $850k). On-the-market at $950k. Final: $997k. Multiple genuine bidders.
