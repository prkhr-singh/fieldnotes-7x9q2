## Quick Verdict

This is a better **location** property than 1 Buick Crescent, but not a better **layout-for-you** property. The home is single-level, bright, well maintained, and has a genuinely strong kitchen and north-facing lounge. It is also far better positioned for Heidelberg hospitals, Burgundy Street, Heidelberg Station and La Trobe.

The problem is your personal criteria: you want spacious rooms, a spacious ensuite, a study/work area, shared bathroom logic and strong kitchen storage. This property wins on kitchen storage and shared bathroom logic, but loses on study/work area and room spaciousness. Bedroom 3 is small enough that it is more like a nursery or compact office than a proper bedroom.

**Overall fit score:** 76 / 100  
**Recommendation:** Consider only if the Heidelberg Heights location premium is worth giving up the huge dedicated study/retreat that Buick has.

## Photo-Based Condition and Age Read

### Exterior

This reads as an older brick single-level home that has been substantially refreshed and carefully maintained. The front garden, hedging and facade are mature rather than new-build. Cosmetic presentation is strong, but the house does not read as new. Inspect roof age, gutters, brickwork movement, drainage, tree-root proximity and garage door operation.

### Lounge

The lounge looks bright, simple and comfortable, with good proportions and a large north-facing window. Finish quality is clean rather than high-end: hybrid flooring, white walls, recessed lighting, split system and security shutter/window treatment. This is a practical room.

### Kitchen

The kitchen is the strongest part of the house for your preferences. It has a U/three-sided working layout, good pantry storage, substantial drawer storage, decent appliance placement and visual connection to the dining/living area. It is not a showpiece island kitchen, but it is probably more storage-efficient than many prettier kitchens.

### Bedrooms

The main bedroom looks clean and calm but not spacious. The second bedroom should work for a child/guest. The third bedroom is the pressure point: at 7.5m2, it can work as a small child's room or compact office, but it should not be counted as a spacious bedroom.

### Bathrooms

The ensuite looks contemporary and well presented, with a small vanity and shower. The finish is good, but the footprint is compact. The central bathroom is not shown as clearly in the downloaded photo set, so verify shower/bath arrangement, ventilation, tile condition and storage in person.

### Outdoor Space

The rear garden is attractive: split-level deck, greenery, firepit area and privacy. It is less "lock and leave" than Buick. If you enjoy small garden upkeep, it is a positive. If you want minimal maintenance, Buick's alfresco is better.

3. Main bedroom and ensuite are not as generous as your ideal.
4. The garden is more beautiful than effortless; maintenance burden is higher than Buick.

## Inspection Checklist

- Confirm whether there is any owners corporation, shared driveway, easement or unit-style title issue despite being marketed as a house.
- Stand in Bedroom 3 and decide whether it works as a real bedroom or only as a study/nursery.
- Check if a proper WFH desk can fit without sacrificing a bedroom.
- Open all pantry/cabinet drawers and confirm kitchen storage depth.
- Check ensuite elbow room, shower size, vanity storage and ventilation.
- Confirm rear garden maintenance expectations: hedges, watering, tree roots, deck treatment.
- Check drainage around the deck and rear garden.
- Inspect roof/gutters, brick movement and damp signs.
- Confirm garage dimensions with both cars/storage needs.
- Verify auction result/current availability before organising further checks.

## Final Rating

**Fit score: 76 / 100**

This is a good property, but not the best fit for your stated priorities. It is a sensible buy if the Heidelberg Heights location is the deciding factor. If the decision is based on internal space, study/work area, and low-maintenance lifestyle, 1 Buick Crescent remains the stronger match.
