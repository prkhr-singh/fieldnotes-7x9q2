## First Impression

Strong on light and views. North orientation is visibly better maximized here than at Landes — multiple decks capture the outlook and the elevated Highton position is felt immediately. The vacancy of the property (no furniture, no personal items) made it feel clinical but also easier to assess the bones.

Post-visit consensus: good views, good price, good natural light, low maintenance, decent backyard. The finishing quality and room sizing were weaker than ideal — buyers would have preferred fewer but larger rooms over the current layout.

## Room-by-Room Notes

**Decks / outdoor:** Multiple decks are a genuine positive. Outlook from elevated position confirmed. The connection between indoor living and outdoor deck is a meaningful lifestyle feature.

**Powder room:** Present — a practical addition for guests / daily use that was not confirmed pre-visit.

**Storage:** Somewhat limited in parts of the house. Not a critical failure but a real compromise vs a purpose-designed layout.

**Natural light:** Noticeably good. North orientation is well-utilized in contrast to Landes where the neighbor clips the light path. This was the standout positive difference from the first stop.

**Layout / room sizing:** The existing offer framing started to clarify the value: buyers would have preferred fewer but larger rooms. The layout is functional but not optimal for the 4-bed format — some rooms felt undersized relative to the footprint.

**Finishing quality:** Below expectations. Not at the standard that would justify a premium over the council CIV. Consistent with an 11-year-old investor-held property where maintenance was adequate but not owner-occupier quality.

**Heating / cooling:** Not prominently noted — the property is newer (2015) and a split-system or ducted setup likely exists. Interior did not present as cold in the way Landes did. Confirm on follow-up.

## Seller Story and Market Context

- Property is vacant. Agent framing: "just got to sell it."
- On market approximately 3 months. Original asking price: $920k. Price has dropped materially from that point.
- Existing offer at $760k on the table — this is a significant anchor. The floor of the market's current view of this property is $760k.
- Seller would accept 60 days settlement; 30 days would be more attractive to them.
- Neighboring comparable property reportedly sold for $800k — agent cited this as a value reference.
- Estimated rental yield: $680–700/week. Area framed as "average house value around $1.5M" with one $3M local sale cited — agent was positioning the area, not a direct comp.

**Sensitive disclosure:** The previous occupant died from illness but NOT in the house. The family subsequently chose not to live in the property. The corporate trust (Johnstone Group Pty Ltd as Trustee for Johnstone Investment Trust) has held it vacant since. This explains the current vendor motivation ("just got to sell it"), the vacancy, and the price drop from $920k.

**Conflict with agent's stated ownership story:** The agent described the original owner as having "bought it new and lived in it the whole time." This conflicts with the contract-confirmed vendor being a corporate trust registered in October 2015. Possible reconciliation: the trust may have been set up by an individual who did live there, or the agent is simplifying / unaware of the full ownership structure. The death disclosure and the corporate trust structure together suggest the property passed through an estate/family trust arrangement. This needs clarification — ask the agent directly.

**B&P:** Not available from the vendor side. Independent inspection encouraged by agent.

## What AI Got Wrong / Missed

**Did not anticipate the sensitive disclosure:** The death of a previous occupant (not in the house) is a legal disclosure requirement in Victoria and explains the trust structure and vacancy better than investor deferred maintenance alone. This is important context for offer strategy — some buyers will walk away on stigma grounds; others will treat it as irrelevant. For this household, the decision should be made explicitly.

**Finishing quality underestimated as a risk:** The pre-visit assessment noted investor-hold maintenance risk but did not flag that the finishing quality of the dwelling itself (not just structural condition) might be below expectations. Buyers noted it on the day.

**Layout not flagged as a compromise:** Room sizing and the "fewer but larger" preference came through clearly in person. The 4-bed 2-storey layout was expected to be a strength — it is, for WFH and flexibility, but the individual room sizes disappointed.
