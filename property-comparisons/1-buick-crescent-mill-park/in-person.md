# In-Person Assessment: 1 Buick Crescent, Mill Park

## First Impression

"Badhiya laga" — strong positive overall. Felt like very good value for the $800k range. The quality of finishes and the functional layout came through in person in a way listing photos don't fully convey.

## Room-by-Room Notes

**Master bedroom:** Dimensions (3.3 × 4.6m) felt like decent size in person. Not oversized but not cramped. Comfortable.

**Ensuite:** Renovated and clean. Compact. Not a standout but not a concern.

**Walk-in / built-in robe:** **Standout positive.** Described as very large. "Itna space to chahiye aur kya chahiye" — felt like it already covers all storage needs with nothing extra required. The size of the WIR meaningfully changes how the master zone feels functionally.

**Bedroom 2 / 3:** Not noted specifically. Likely felt functional based on no negative comments.

**Study / retreat (upstairs, 5.1 × 4.8m):** Felt slightly excessive alongside the lounge — two large multi-purpose zones created a sense of "too much space for how we live." Not a dealbreaker. But the sheer size (24.5m²) is more than needed for a WFH study; it's closer to a second living room.

**Main living / lounge:** Also felt oversized in combination with the retreat. Both zones together felt like more living space than a small family would use daily.

**Kitchen:** Not specifically commented on in-person (AI scored 8/10 — likely confirmed).

**Laundry:** **Specifically and positively called out.** One of the strongest functional spaces in the house. Separate, well-sized. This was not an expected highlight.

**Outdoor / alfresco:** Large, well-maintained. Inspected the back. Confirmed the covered alfresco is properly covered (not open deck). Felt good. Seasonal limitation (Melbourne ~180 outdoor days) noted but the heater makes it more year-round than an open deck.

**Under-stair storage:** Noted as existing. Was closed on the day — not inspected. Check on next visit.

**Garage / parking:** Space for 3 cars noted. Overall size judged appropriate.

## What AI Got Wrong / Missed

1. **Walk-in robe quality:** AI captured it as part of `room_spaciousness_score` (8/10) but the WIR was the single most-noted positive of the visit. It deserves separate recognition. AI didn't have a WIR-specific score.
2. **Laundry room quality:** Completely absent from the AI rubric. Emerged as a meaningful positive in person.
3. **"Two living zones" effect:** AI scored study (10/10) and lounge separately. In person the combination felt like "too much space" for a small household. AI cannot easily assess this — it scores zones individually, not how they feel together.
4. **Under-stair storage:** Not in AI assessment at all. Exists. Needs physical inspection.
