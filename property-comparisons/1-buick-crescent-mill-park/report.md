## Photo-Based Condition and Age Read

### Exterior

The facade presents as heavily refreshed: grey render/cladding, white shutters, modern garage door, clean roofline and neat low-maintenance landscaping. The underlying building is older, but visually it reads as a renovated 1990s house rather than a tired original home. Check in person for roof tile age, gutter condition, downpipe drainage, eave condition and any movement/cracking around the render.

### Living Areas

The lounge and dining/kitchen areas look bright, polished and well maintained. Spotted gum-style timber floors appear in good cosmetic condition. The ground lounge has high ceilings, downlights, split system and direct alfresco connection. It looks genuinely usable, not just staged for photos.

### Kitchen

The kitchen looks like a substantial renovation rather than original cabinetry. Positives: 900mm oven/cooktop, dishwasher, broad fridge space, tall pantry/cabinet bank, overhead cabinets, under-bench drawers, farmhouse sink and stone-look counters. The limitation is layout: it is a perimeter kitchen, not an island kitchen, so prep space and traffic flow should be tested during inspection.

### Bathrooms

Both bathrooms look renovated with white subway tiles and patterned floors. The central bathroom is stronger than the ensuite: it has a freestanding bath, separate shower and more generous floor feel. The ensuite is clean and modern but compact. Inspect tile falls, grout, silicone, exhaust fan, water pressure and any signs of prior moisture because photo presentation can hide wet-area risk.

### Bedrooms and Study

The master looks comfortable and benefits from upstairs privacy. The retreat/study is outstanding for your work use case: large, bright, flexible and already shown as family/work storage space. The two downstairs bedrooms are fine but not spacious; their strength is having robes and direct access to the central bathroom zone.

### Alfresco

The alfresco is a major lifestyle fit. It is covered, private, has a built-in/outdoor kitchen zone, heater, dining area and lounge seating. This gives outdoor functionality without the maintenance burden of a large lawn.

## Buyer-Specific Risks

1. Ensuite may disappoint if "spacious ensuite" is a hard requirement.
2. Secondary bedrooms are not large; measure furniture fit for future child/guest use.
3. Small 303m2 land means land-value upside is weaker than bigger-block alternatives.
4. Two-storey layout is great for separation but creates stair dependency.
5. Strong photo staging may be hiding smaller defects. Building/pest remains mandatory.

## Inspection Checklist

- Confirm actual orientation with phone compass.
- Stand in the lounge, dining, retreat and master with lights off; assess natural light honestly.
- Check whether the ensuite feels cramped when the door/shower are open.
- Open every kitchen cabinet and pantry cabinet; check usable storage depth.
- Measure the garage width with your actual car in mind.
- Check timber floors for cupping, gaps, scratches and hollow spots.
- Inspect wet areas for silicone condition, grout cracks, exhaust, water pressure and drainage speed.
- Inspect alfresco roof flashing, drainage, timber deck condition and heater condition.
- Ask whether alfresco appliances/heater/fireplace are included in sale.
- Commission building/pest before auction.
