## Quick Verdict

2A Macadam is charming and visually appealing, but it is not a strong match for your current home-buying criteria. It is better read as a lifestyle cottage than a practical main-home candidate for this search.

The key misses are location/commute, bathroom count, family-space depth and resale/use-case alignment. The $825k price may sit under the FHBG cap, but affordability alone does not make it a good buy if the property does not meet the core brief.

## Photo Read

Photos show a renovated period cottage with strong presentation: white weatherboard, red roof, polished dark floors, updated kitchen and a character bathroom. It feels warm, finished and photogenic.

That same charm is also the limitation. The rooms feel cottage-scaled, the bathroom count is thin, and the home does not solve the daily family/work-space needs as well as Mill Park options.

## Price Lens

The price is not the primary issue; mismatch is. At $825k, you would be spending similar money to stronger current-home candidates while accepting a less practical location and fewer bathrooms.

## Bottom Line

Do not pursue this for the current search unless the strategy changes toward lifestyle/investment or a Daylesford-specific plan.
