## Orientation and Light

The downloaded floorplan does not include a north arrow, so this is lower confidence than the properties where the floorplan gave a clear compass. The property sits on Cardinal Close with the driveway/frontage toward the cul-de-sac and a long side verandah along one side of the home.

What can be inferred from photos:

- Living and dining have large windows/sliders and appear bright.
- Secondary bedrooms have decent-size windows and should not feel cave-like.
- The verandah roof may shade one side of the house. This is good in summer but may reduce winter sun into adjacent rooms.
- If the large lawn/back garden receives strong north/west light, the outdoor area will be warm and usable but also hotter/drier in summer.

Inspection check: stand in the living room, family/meals area, master and verandah; use phone compass and note which windows face north, west and south. The seasonal-light rating should be adjusted after that.

## Inspection Checklist

- Confirm whether the yard feels like a joy or a burden.
- Measure mental desk fit in Bed 2 and Bed 3.
- Check if the master WIR actually removes the need for extra bedroom furniture.
- Stand in the ensuite and test elbow room around vanity, toilet and shower door.
- Open every pantry, linen, robe and laundry cupboard.
- Inspect the verandah: roof condition, leaks, drainage, wind exposure and year-round usability.
- Check timber floors for bounce/squeak, window operation, damp smells and visible cracks.
- Ask for age of roof, gutters, hot water, ducted heating/cooling, switchboard and any renovations.
- Ask why the vendors are selling below the 2023 sale price guide and whether pre-auction offers are considered.

It is not a better lifestyle fit than Buick because Buick gives a stronger WFH zone, lower-maintenance land, better covered alfresco fit and more usable internal functionality despite smaller land.
