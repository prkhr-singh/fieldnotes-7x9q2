## Quick Verdict

11 Archer is one of the more useful Mill Park layouts in the new batch: three bedrooms, a true study, two generous living zones, a large double garage and a covered pergola. It passes many of your daily-life criteria better than the lower-priced older houses.

The problem is price. The $850k-$925k guide already sits above your practical comfort band, and an EOI/private campaign can still transact above the top of guide if multiple buyers engage. It is worth inspecting only with a hard ceiling in the low $900s and a willingness to walk away before the FHBG cap is threatened.

## Photo Read

The house looks well kept but older. The polished timber floors, arched brickwork, timber kitchen and brick facade point to an established family home rather than a newer low-maintenance build. The interiors photograph bright and spacious, helped by staging and large windows. Bathrooms look serviceable rather than luxury; the kitchen is usable but dated.

The strongest visual assets are the living room scale, the dedicated study, the covered pergola and the oversized garage/workshop feel. The main caution is that a lot of the property value is in land and older-house character rather than the newer compact layout you usually prefer.

## Price Lens

The guide is $850k-$925k. For this search, that creates three bid zones:

| Zone | Read |
|---|---|
| Below $890k | Attractive if building/pest is clean, but this may be unrealistic. |
| $890k-$925k | Fair if you love the layout, but it is already above comfort. |
| Above $925k | Only consider if it clearly beats Buick/Hartley in person and remains under FHBG. |

This should not be chased to $950k. Even if technically inside the FHBG cap, it reduces cash buffer and leaves less room for maintenance on an older, larger-block property.
