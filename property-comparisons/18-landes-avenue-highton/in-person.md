## First Impression

"Pleasantly surprised" — maintenance quality felt materially better than expected from listing photos. The house does not have strong architectural personality but the practical fit for a couple was immediately clear. Location, views, and proximity to shops registered positively within the first few minutes.

Interior felt noticeably cold — enough to stand out as a downside on a mild autumn day. Not a deal-breaker by itself but flags that the heating setup needs attention before winter.

## Room-by-Room Notes

**Natural light / orientation:** North orientation exists but a neighboring larger house blocks a significant portion of the sun path. Summer light still reaches the interior to some degree; winter gain will be weaker. This is the single most meaningful compromise at this property.

**Veranda / enclosed outdoor area:** A genuine positive. The covered outdoor space functions as a secondary living/hosting area. Blinds clip down in winter. The coverage does reduce north light into the main living zone — the two trade-offs (usable outdoor space vs interior light) are directly linked.

**Kitchen:** Storage was liked. Practical layout, functional bench space. No major gap noted.

**Views:** Decent views from two rooms. Elevated Highton position delivers on this front.

**Heating / cooling:** Split-system air conditioning confirmed. Agent stated it regulates temperature well and the cooling load is manageable on hot days. No ducted system. This is a self-funded upgrade if ducted is ever desired (~$10–20k).

**Roof:** Acceptable condition from visible inspection. No immediate concern flagged.

**Stormwater drainage:** Already in place — the veranda addition connects correctly. This was a pre-visit watchpoint (veranda permit / in-ground stormwater) and it was resolved positively.
