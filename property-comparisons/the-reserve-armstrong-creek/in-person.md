## Summary

The Reserve is a ~600-lot estate in Armstrong Creek/Charlemont. Nathan (sales agent) gave a thorough, low-pressure briefing. The numbers work — a 14×32 lot at $432k plus a Metricon Amira 25S build at ~$300k lands at approximately $730k, under the $750k FHBG-eligible ceiling. But the post-visit reaction was mixed-to-negative: the timeline is too long, the land doesn't feel premium, and the value case is not compelling enough to prioritise over the established-home shortlist.

The visit was completed properly — the family had been encouraged to take this lead seriously by Mamu, and they did. The outcome is a respectful "not yet" rather than an outright rejection.

## Nathan's Process Advice

- **Land-first** is the more common sequence: secure the lot, then take site details to multiple builders
- **Design-first** is also viable: find a plan you love, then match a block
- Untitled land creates breathing room — long lead time before needing to finalise builder choice
- Titled land forces faster decisions
- Builder comparison advice: ask what is NOT included, not just what is included
- Large display homes often contain many upgrades — distort expectations
- Build quality across mainstream builders is broadly similar; differences are in inclusions and pricing detail

## First-Home-Buyer Mechanics

- FHBG and stamp duty eligibility depends on both land AND build contracts being signed
- Total project must stay below $750k for stamp duty benefit
- Settlement after title is largely a waiting game
