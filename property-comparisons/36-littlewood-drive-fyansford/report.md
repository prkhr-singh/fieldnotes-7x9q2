## Orientation

The listing/profile evidence available in this pass does not provide a reliable north arrow. Treat orientation as an inspection item, not a settled positive. For this block size, the most important check is whether the kitchen/living glazing faces north/east enough to avoid a dark open-plan room. If the living zone faces west, summer heat and glare will matter because compact estates often have limited eaves/shading.

Inspection check: stand in the living/kitchen and alfresco at the same time of day as a likely daily-use period, turn lights off, and use a phone compass. Reject the orientation story if the main living is south-facing and dependent on artificial lighting.

## Risks And Unknowns

- Fyansford/old quarry estate context: require B&P focus on slab movement, cracking, retaining walls, stormwater, drainage and fill/rock interface.
- Compact-estate design can still mean tight setbacks, limited storage and heat transfer if windows/eaves are poor.
- Verify whether it is a true freestanding title with no owners corporation, shared driveway or estate maintenance obligations.
- No contract/Section 32 reviewed: check covenants, design controls, easements, building approvals and any developer restrictions.
- Confirm NBN technology and mobile coverage for WFH.
