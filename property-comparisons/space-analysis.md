# Space and Room-Size Matrix

**Prepared:** 4 May 2026  
**Scope:** All properties currently listed in `comparison-matrix.csv`  
**Purpose:** Separate spaciousness from overall desirability. Land size tells you maintenance and land value; internal living area and room dimensions tell you daily comfort.

## Size Bands Used

These are practical bands for comparing these listings, not formal valuation standards.

| Space type | Small | Mid | Large | Extra-large |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Bedroom | under 9m2 | 9-12m2 | 12-16m2 | 16m2+ |
| Master bedroom | under 11m2 | 11-14m2 | 14-18m2 | 18m2+ |
| Study / retreat | under 7m2 | 7-12m2 | 12-20m2 | 20m2+ |
| Living room / family | under 14m2 | 14-20m2 | 20-28m2 | 28m2+ |
| Dining | under 9m2 | 9-14m2 | 14m2+ | N/A |

Bathrooms, kitchens, laundries and outdoor spaces are classified qualitatively because most floorplans do not dimension them.

## Practical Interpretation

- **Do not read larger land as more spacious living.** Waurnvale has the largest land, but Northbridge and Arbour may fit your day-to-day life better.
- **Internal area around 140-150m2 is the sweet spot** for the current shortlist when paired with 300m2-ish land.
- **Arbour is the surprise:** it has only 239m2 land, but the room dimensions are strong because the plan is dense and vertical.
- **St Hellier is location-led, not space-led.** It has a good lounge/kitchen but is weakest for WFH and room flexibility.
- **Buick remains the WFH benchmark** because the retreat is unusually large and already validated in person.
