## Auction Pricing — The 10% Rule

**Rule:** In the current Melbourne market (2026), auction clearance prices typically land ~10% above the top of the advertised guide range.

**Empirical validation (2 May 2026):**
- Property: 3-bed/2-bath single-level, Heidelberg Heights / Banyule area
- Advertised guide: $850,000–$900,000
- Auction result: **$997,000** (+10.8% above top of guide)
- Opening call: $860,000 (silence at $850k, one bidder broke deadlock)
- On-the-market call: $950,000
- Final run: $5,000 increments to $991k, then $1,000 increments to $997k

**Application:** Treat the top of any guide as the floor of the real price range, not the ceiling.

| Property | Guide Low | Guide High | Expected clearance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Buick Crescent, Mill Park | $740,000 | $790,000 | ~$869k–$885k |

This means Mill Park likely clears at or above the $880k comfort ceiling. Pre-auction offer is the rational strategy.

## Auction Mechanics

### What Happens on the Day

1. Auctioneer announces property details, settlement terms, deposit requirements
2. Bidding opens — often below guide. First bidder breaks deadlock
3. Momentum builds once bidding opens. Hesitation early is normal.
4. **"On the market"** = vendor's reserve has been met. Sale is certain from this point. At the Heidelberg Heights auction, this was called at $950k.
5. Final stretch: increments tighten from $10k → $5k → $1k near the top
6. Small increments near the top feel psychologically trivial — they are not
7. Hammer falls. Contract and 10% deposit due on the spot.

### The Deposit Requirement

**10% of purchase price is due on signing, on auction day.** This is cash or bank cheque — not a 5% FHBG deposit. The FHBG deposit structure applies to the final loan, not to the auction day mechanics.

At $850k clearance: **$85,000 cash needed on auction day.**

This means auction-day deposit funds must be liquid and available before auction day, not just before settlement.

### Crowd-Reading

- Count genuine bidders early (2 vs 5 changes the signal entirely)
- Late-appearing bidders often stand at the back and only become obvious near the end
- Note where bidding opens vs the guide — calibrates vendor reserve expectations
- "On the market" announcement = reserve cleared, vendor cannot pass in

### Emotional Discipline

- **"Emotional nahi hona hai"** — agree on a hard cap before leaving home that morning
- Do not revise the cap on-site, even if the gap feels like "just $2k"
- Small increments near $1M feel trivial after a long run-up — this is exactly where people break discipline
- Bring a calmer companion who can signal when you're being pulled in
- Auction has deliberate theater: pacing, auctioneer commentary, crowd energy. Recognise it as performative.

## Pre-Auction Offers

**When they make sense:** When a property is right, you've done due diligence, and auction clearance will likely exceed your budget.

**How they work:**
- Approach the agent and ask if the vendor is open to pre-auction offers
- Offer must be unconditional or near-unconditional (building/pest only, no finance condition once pre-approval is in hand)
- Frame with a 24–48 hour expiry — creates urgency without pressure
- Vendor incentive: certainty before auction momentum builds

**What price to offer:** Below likely clearance (so vendor doesn't just pocket the offer and still auction). The sweet spot is: above what the vendor would expect a lowball to be, below the auction clearance estimate. For Buick Crescent ($869k–$885k clearance estimate), a clean offer of $820k–$840k with a 24-hour window is the right range.

**When not to bother:** If the agent says the vendor wants to go to auction and is not entertaining pre-auction offers, respect that and prepare for auction day instead.

## Pricing Psychology

### Guide Ranges Are Not Neutral

Agents typically quote guides below the vendor's reserve and below what they believe the market will clear. Reasons:
- Low guide attracts more foot traffic and more bidders → more auction heat
- Legally, agents must quote within 10% of vendor's reserve (VIC law) — but interpretation is flexible

**As a buyer:** Always price-test from the top of the guide, not the bottom.

### Life-Stage Fit Affects Competition

At the Heidelberg Heights auction, the winner appeared to be a later-life couple (kids moved out, ~10-year horizon). They likely valued the low-maintenance, single-level layout more than buyers with growing families. When inspecting a property, ask: who else is competing for this? Life-stage fit of competing buyers affects bid aggressiveness.

### Indian Buyer Market Dynamics

Open, spacious layouts are especially appealing to many Indian buyers. This means double-storey homes with large open-plan living and generous room sizes attract more competition from this demographic in Melbourne's north corridor. Worth factoring into demand estimates.

## Walk-Away Price Discipline

**The core question for any target property:** At what price does the currently considered property stop being a good deal — relative to the alternative of building elsewhere?

For Buick Crescent, the walk-away price must be explicitly set before auction day, never revised on-site. Key anchors:

- In-person price ceiling established: **$830,000** (from 2 May inspection)
- 10% auction clearance estimate: **$869k–$885k**
- H&L total cost equivalent (Carlisle Inspire 23-variant + $400–500k land): **~$715–830k**
- If Buick clears above $850k, the H&L alternative becomes cost-comparable with a brand-new, larger-spec home

**Rule:** Set one number. Write it down before leaving home on auction day. Do not revise at the venue regardless of momentum.

## Quick Filters Learned from Experience

| Pattern | Rule |
|---|---|
| "X/Y Street" address | Unit/townhouse. Always reject. Body corporate, no land ratio. |
| Guide $900k+ in Whittlesea LGA | Expect clearance above $950k — likely above FHBG cap |
| Single-storey 1970s–1980s | Small floor plate (120–145m²), asbestos risk, renovation burden |
| "Boutique rental opportunity" in listing text | Investor-facing property; likely smaller or in higher-turnover area |
| Open deck as primary outdoor feature | ~180 days usable. Don't pay a premium for this specifically. |
| Guide top $790k | Expect clearance ~$870k–$885k |
